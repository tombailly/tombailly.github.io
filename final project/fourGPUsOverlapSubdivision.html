Overlap and Subdivision are pretty much the essential yin and yang of reality. You can't have one without the other, and you can't have reality without either of these patterns.

Here are some examples:

Fish scales and roof shingles are all subdivided modules arranged in an overlapping pattern to achieve certain characterisics.

We make analogies based on something we already know to learn new concepts in other disciplines - total OVERLAP!

Geodesic domes (some of the most space efficient structures engineered so far) are created by subdividing the triangular faces of the regular polyhedra, tetrahedron, octahedron, and icosahedron. Those flat triangular faces then overlap to create a spherical volume!

Doing functional exercises in the gym, like ball slams, have overlapping carry-over to real life situations (see video directly below)!
