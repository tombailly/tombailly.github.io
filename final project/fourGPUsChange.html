From the moment we enter crying to the moment we leave dying, we are in a state of constant change. And so is everything else in reality. The air that breezes past your skin is different, and so is the surface of your skin, the instant that first moment is over.

Ideas, politics, knowledge bases change constantly. We're always re-defining notions of equal rights, our interpretations of of the physical world (the world used to be "flat" until the scope of our understanding changed.

Our diets and physical practices change throughout our life, typically out of necessity or desire to achieve a goal.

Change really only stops when your dead. And even then, your physical body changes into other forms of energy for the physical world to use.

"True change and higher human adaptation are not made by resistance to the old habits. Change is not a matter of not doing something; it is a matter of doing something else."
